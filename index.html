What Are Neutrino Oscillations ?


    Neutrinos are unique amongst the particles of the standard model as they can change their nature. This is known as neutrino oscillations, when
    a neutrinos flavour changes whilst travelling through space. This is due to a mismatch in quantum states when propagating through free space
    as the mass eigenstate dosent match the flavour eigenstate resulting in this change in nature. For example, when an electron lepton neutrino 
    produced from the sun propagates freely in space, its flavour composition changes due to the flavour eigenstate not having a definitive mass
    as it’s too small. This premise explains the basis of neutrino oscillations and how they happen. Neutrino oscillations can be modelled by the
    Pontectorvo-Maki-Nakagawa-Sakata matrix (PMNS) which is a lepton/neutrino mixing unitary matrix essentially showing the different probabilities of 
    finding a neutrino of a specific flavour. its a 3x3 unitary matrix which means that the sum of the probabilities of finding a particular 
    flavour add up to 1. This behaviour is similar to that of schrodinger's cat in a box thought experiment in the sense each electron, muon or tau
    neutrino could simultaneously have a mass of m1, m2 or m3 until the mass is actually measured and determined just like how the cat is thought
    to dead and alive simultaneously until the box is opened. The T2K Neutrino oscillation experiment provides information regarding mixing angles 
    and mass-squared differences which can be used to reconstruct the matrix. The mixing angles determines the diffrent amplitudes of the neutrino
    oscillations and how the diffrent flavour states are mixed together and the mass squared diffreneces refer to the diffrences in the square
    of the 3 mass eigenstates. Furthermore, There's also a cp violation phase within the matrix which explains how neutrinos with definite
    eigenstates of flavour (electron, muon, tau), don't have corresponding definite eigenstates of mass allowing for this phenomenon to occur. 
    It explains the diffrences between the behaviour of neutrinos and anti-neutrinos implying that oscillation probabilites for neutrinos would 
    be diffrent to that of anti-neutrinos. (GISGeography,2023)

Experiments evolving our understanding of neutrinos

      the homestake experiment was crucial in the development of discovering neutrino oscillations as it provided the first
      experimental evidence for the solar neutrino problem. The experiment invovled using a large underground chlorine-based
      liquid tank which contained chlorine-37 atoms. These chlorine atoms would interact with the solar neutrinos resulting
      in argon-37 atoms to be formed in turn showing how many solar neutrinos were detected. This resulted in only approximately
      a third of flux neutrinos observed based on what was expected from the theoretical models. The experiment was conducted by 
      Raymond Davis in the 1960s to early 1990s postulating further neutrino experiments and resulting in Raymond receiving a 
      nobel prize in 2002 signifying the importance of his work. 

      The SuperKamiokande experiment was the first experiment to provide evidence for neutrino oscilllations. Its purpose was to 
      find diffrent properties of neutrinos which inculded their masses and measuring and refining the mixing angles. the three 
      predominate mixing angles which parametises the PMNS matrix are θ12,θ23,θ13 as well as a cp violating phase δcp. θ12 
      represents the mixing between the electron letpon neutrinos flavour state and mass egienstates. θ23 shows the mixing 
      betweenthe muon and tau neutrinos. θ13 shows the mixing between all 3 flavours of neutrinos. The SuperKamiokande is a
      50 kiloton water Cherenkov detector as show in figure 1. When a charged particle is produced from a neutrino interaction,
      if the speed of the charged particle is faster than the speed of light within the water, this will produce cherenkov radiation
      in the form of coned shaped flashes of blue light. In doing so were able to use photomultiplier tubes (PMTs) which are within 
      the SuperKamiokande to measuere the intensity as well as the timing of the flashes of blue light allowing us to recostruct 
      the trajectory and find the energies of these particles  impling the type of neutrino.

What Are Neutrino Oscillations ?

’.

Neutrinos are unique amongst the particles of the standard model as they can change their nature. This is known as neutrino oscillations, when a neutrinos flavour changes whilst travelling through space. This is due to a mismatch in quantum states when propagating through free space as the mass eigenstate dosent match the flavour eigenstate resulting in this change in nature. For example, when an electron lepton neutrino produced from the sun propagates freely in space, its flavour composition changes due to the flavour eigenstate not having a definitive mass as it’s too small. This premise explains the basis of neutrino oscillations and how they happen. Neutrino oscillations can be modelled by the Pontectorvo-Maki-Nakagawa-Sakata matrix which is a quark mixing unitary matrix essentially showing the different probabilities of finding a neutrino of a specific flavour. The matrix being unitary ensures that the sum of the probabilities of finding a particular flavour add up to 1. This behaviour is similar to that of schrodinger's cat in a box thought experiment in the sense each electron, muon or tau neutrino could simultaneously have a mass of m1, m2 or m3 until the mass is actually measured and determined just like how the cat is thought to dead and alive simultaneously until the box is opened. The T2K Neutrino oscillation experiment provides information regarding mixing angles and mass-squared differences which can be used to reconstruct the matrix. There's also a cp violation phase within the matrix which ultimately explains how neutrinos with definite eigenstates of flavour (electron, muon, tau), don't have corresponding definite eigenstates of mass allowing for this phenomenon to occur. talk about changes in flavour and how the mass eigenstate doesn't match the flavour eigenstate the type of neutrino changes.
